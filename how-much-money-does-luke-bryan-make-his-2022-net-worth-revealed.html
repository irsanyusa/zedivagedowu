With a number of singles parading in Billboard music charts and previously successful tours, Luke Bryan surely does earn money. He has consistently been one of the highest paid musicians in the world. This brings us to the question: How much does Luke Bryan make?

Luke Bryan Chiefly Earns Through Music

It didn’t take long for Luke Bryan to score a songwriting deal when he moved to Nashville, Tennessee. He entered the music industry as a songwriter for Travis Tritt and Billy Currington. Apart from that, he performed in a club where an A&R representative at Capitol Records offered him a contract after hearing him sing. There, his rise in country music began.

Soon after its release, Bryan’s debut single “All My Friends Say” made it to No. 5 on the Hot Country Songs chart. He released his first album I’ll Stay Me in 2007, with two out of his 12 tracks landing in charts again. Since this debut, his subsequent singles have peaked in country music charts and Billboard charts, several reaching No.1.

His fifth studio album, Kill The Lights, launched at No. 1 on Billboard Hot 200. In addition, all six of his singles from this album ranked No. 1 on the Billboard Country Airplay Chart – a record breaking achievement in Billboard history.

What is His Net Worth?

While he largely makes money through his music creations, Bryan has also made bank on other projects and commercial ventures. He made an appearance in Celebrity Apprentice alongside country musician Emily West. He also joined the panel of judges of American Idol from season 16 to 18. Lastly, he is a co-owner of Buck Commander, a company that sells deer hunting gear and equipment.

With all this musical and business ventures topping up his bank account, how much does this country artist really earn? According to Celebrity Net Worth, Luke Bryan’s wealth has racked up to $160 million this year.

As a judge in American Idol, Bryan approximately received $12 million per year. Reportedly, when he hits the road for tours, he earns $1 million per show. From his endeavors since June 2017 to June 2020, he has obtained roughly $138 million.

In relation to this, Bryan has the fatherly responsibility to raise his two sons with Caroline Boyer. In 2007, his sister Kelly has unexpectedly died for unknown causes. Unfortunately, Kelly’s husband Ben passed away in 2014. As an aftermath, Bryan and Boyer made the decision to support their nephew and two nieces.
