Bella Hadid Stands Behind Ariana Grande in Her Plea to Stop Body Shaming on Social Media

 Posted on September 1, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  435 words  |  Larita Shotwell

In a recent TikTok video, Ariana Grande opened up about the negative impact of body shaming on social media. The pop star called on her followers to think twice before commenting on someone’s body or shape, as these words can have a significant impact on someone’s mental health. And Bella Hadid decided to support her.

Ariana Grande is against body shaming.

Grande’s message is especially important in today’s digital age, where social media can be a breeding ground for hurtful comments and unrealistic beauty standards. Body shaming can lead to low self-esteem, anxiety, and depression, and it’s crucial to be mindful of the words we use when discussing someone’s appearance.

Grande’s call to action is a reminder that kindness and empathy should always come first, both online and offline. We should aim to create a culture of body positivity and support, where individuals are celebrated for their unique qualities rather than criticized for their appearance.

Ariana Grande’s message is a powerful reminder of the impact that our words can have on others. By being mindful of our language and promoting body positivity, we can create a safer and more supportive online community for everyone.

Grande had bad times when everyone thought she was okay.

“I think we should be gentle and less comfortable commenting on people’s bodies, no matter what. If you think you’re saying something good or well-intentioned, whatever it is: healthy, unhealthy, big, small, this, that.”

In a TikTok video, Grande confessed that she had taken lots of antidepressants mixed with bad habits and had hardly eaten. At that time, people thought she was okay and healthy. Ariana literally opened her soul to the public with a plea for people to be kinder and stop shaming each other.

Bella Hadid decides to support Grande to stop body shaming.

Immediately after her video, Bella Hadid made a post on Instagram to support Ariana and the idea of how people influence our mental health by trying to impose standards about our appearance.

“You never know what someone is dealing with mentally or physically. Disease or depression. Grief or heartache. You just don’t, and you will never know until you walk a day in their shoes. Instead of unsolicited advice or opinions, judgment or aggression, just try to offer a helping hand and be kind.”

Bella Hadid emphasized that there is always a reason why people look the way they do and that we should be kinder and more compassionate toward ourselves and others.

Hadid also criticized social media platforms like Instagram for promoting unrealistic beauty standards and creating a culture of comparison and insecurity. She urged her followers to remember that these platforms are not real, and that we should focus on cultivating genuine connections and positive relationships with ourselves and others.

Hadid’s message is particularly relevant in today’s society, where social media can have a significant impact on mental health and body image. By promoting body positivity and self-love, we can create a healthier and more supportive culture that celebrates diversity and individuality.

Instead of judgment, let’s try to give a helping hand. Morality is a set of beliefs. When we judge others and ourselves, it is about values. But values can be different.
